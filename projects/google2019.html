Google 1-Day Design Challenge

This was a design challenge I did for a Google interview which I completed in a single 8 hour day. The prompt was "Design a tool that helps student discover events to go to at your school."

I began by formulating some design requirements:

MY ROLE
  • UI Design
  • Visual Design
OBJECTIVES
  • Ensure users can discover a variety of events in various categories and filter between them.
  • Discovery view which shows featured images of events in the upcoming week and promotes interest.
  • Calendar view which provides more detail on times of events.
  • Map view which shows where events are on campus.
  • Detail modal which allows users to discover more detail on the events including a description, and a button to add to their calendar app.
  • Fully responsive design that works on all screen sizes.
  • Consistent with Texas A&M branding.

WIREFRAMES

I am a big sketching advocate and always like to sketch wireframes before jumping on to the computer. It keeps the ideas flexible and allows me to have a gameplan before jumping in to software (typically a tool like Illustrator or XD)

In these wires I was sure to focus on how the user experience would differ on mobile and tablet / desktop sized screens.

STYLE GUIDE

Establishing a specific color scheme and typography, along with iconography early on in the design process is ideal. For this challenge I just threw something simple together loosely based on Texas A&M's branding guidelines.

MOCKUPS

DISCOVERY VIEW

This view allows users to discover activities they may be interested in in the upcoming week. They can filter different categories by simply tapping them in the sidebar.

CALENDAR VIEW

The calendar view offers a more detailed view of the events and their respective times. This design is similiar to existing calendar apps such as Google Calendar which makes it familiar and intuitive.

MAP VIEW

The map views shows the campus and where events are located on the campus. In all 3 views, a user can tap an event to pull up a modal which offers more detail on the event.
