PIXL Simulator - Data Visualization Interface for Mars Rover

PIXL Simulator is a tool I built for the Aoelian Lab at Texas A&M University. It is part of a larger project funded by the National Science Foundation and NASA. This tool simulates what it will be like to recieve data from the PIXL (X-ray Fluorescence) instrument on board the new Mars Perseverance rover. This instrument will discover details about the chemical composition of soil on the Martian soil, possibly revealing evidence of life.

I had to gain some specialized knowledge in geology and astrogeology in order to design the tool which utilizes soil samples from Earth (XRF scans) to simulate what the PIXL instrument will do. This tool will be used for training purposes so that scientists will know what to expect when they use the PIXL instrument.
